The girls got to dress up in their Halloween costume for dance class. It was so adorable to see all the little girls dressed up in their costumes.

This year Laney wanted to be Buzz Lightyear. Kenzie couldn't make up her mind so I made her stick with a cowgirl. I thought cowgirl would be cute since her kindergarten classroom is a cowboy/cowgirl theme and she likes Jessie from Toy Story. And I planned on Lai Lai wearing a chicken costume that Laney wore two years ago. It turned to be a Toy Story 2 theme - Laney as Buzz, Kenzie as Jessie and Lai Lai as Al the Chicken Man from Al's Toy Barn.

So looking at the cost of costumes, it was crazy expensive from the Disney Store. Even the "cheap" ones from Wal-Mart were still $20 for a printed Buzz Lightyear jumpsuit without wings in cheap material. And the Jessie costume was $35 for shirt and pants, $25 for the hat and $25 for shoes. Um yeah. I'm not spending that kind of money for a Halloween costume that will only be worn once a year (because I was already spending more than that for their dance recital costumes that will only be worn once a year).

I decided I would make the Jessie costume. I bought a pair of jeans at Goodwill for $3, a white button up shirt for $2, fabric and tassels for $8 and a red cowgirl hat for $6. It was also fun for me to figure out how to put it together. It worked out pretty well. We ended up borrowing Buzz Lightyear from a friend who had a son who was Buzz last year. It was the nice costume from the Disney Store. Laney loved it! Buzz is her favorite character from Toy Story.

Side note: As mentioned before, this year having 2 girls in dance class means big cost for us. Kenzie is in a class where she is learning ballet, tap and hip-hop. She will be doing 3 numbers at the recital, one for each genre. Her costumes cost $110 and Laney's was $50. Yeah, we just spent $160 on dance costumes that will only be worn once. I told Nate that this is like buying a prom dress! Crazy.
