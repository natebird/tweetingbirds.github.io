Chinese New Year was Feb. 7 this year, the year of the golden rat. And every year for the last two years (this was our third year) we have been going to the Chinese New Year Festival. There at the cultural plaza, they have a dragon dance outside to bring good luck into the new year. Then there are performances by dancers, singer, karate performers, and drummers on stage inside the conference center. But best of all, the conference center is lined with booths of authentic Chinese food. Food I remember eating when I was a little girl living in Taiwan. I love going once a year because it bring back memories of yummy food from my childhood -- Chinese sausages, white pepper seasoned fried chicken, red bean cakes, bubble drinks, milky shave ice with fruit toppings, chow mien, egg rolls, zong zi, and so much more.
