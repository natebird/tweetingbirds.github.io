It finally snowed here! And this time, it's pretty heavy and painful snow. Painful because it's more icy than fluffy, so when it hits you, it hurts a bit. We are glad to be getting any kind of precipitation since we are still in a sever drought. The snow began around 4:25 p.m. and about four hours later we have an inch sitting outside. Here are pictures taken of Kenzie in the snow. We didn't keep her out too long since she has been fighting a cold the past few days. Once again, she is teething, more molars, and the symptoms include runny nose (now it has traveled to her throat and into a cough), irritability, whininess, drooling, and a constant desire to stick her fingers in her mouth or chew on anything rubbery.

Anyway, back to the snow. Us, Southerners, don't know how to deal with the snow... meaning we don't know how to drive in this weather condition. So everything is closed -- churches, schools, work, stores, youth activities, flights canceled, etc. Having lived in Hawaii and Georgia for the majority of my childhood, you can imagine the shock I received when I went out to BYU-Provo for college and nothing EVER canceled due to snow or cold weather! "You mean I have to walk to school in 2-3 feet of snow?! But my shoes and clothes are going to get wet!"
