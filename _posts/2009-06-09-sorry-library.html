We borrowed Quick Draw Fairies and Princesses book from the library last Tuesday. Kenzie has been excited to draw the mermaid in the book. So before nap time last Wednesday, I got a piece of paper, pencil, and the book and placed it on Kenzie's bed for us to draw something together. I had Laney in one arm ready for nap. I decided to put Laney down in the crib first that ways I would have my hands free for drawing. Before leaving Kenzie's room, the thought came into mind that I probably shouldn't leave Kenzie with a book about drawing and a pencil. Especially since earlier when we were talking about drawing princesses out of that book, she asked why we needed pieces of paper.

"Don't draw in the library book," I said firmly to Kenzie. "I'm going to put Laney down in her crib and I'll be right back. DO NOT draw in the library book."

I was gone less than a minute. Came back to find Kenzie drawing with fierce speed in the library book on the mermaid page. Of course, I scolded her and reiterated that we don't draw in library books. She said, "But I was trying to draw princesses for us, Mom. I was trying to draw fast." And yup. Flipping through some of the other pages I found more doodles. A total of eight pages in the book. Luckily it was in pencil, minimal damage done.


I told Nate about the incident and we had the following conversations with Kenzie about it.

Kenzie: I drew in the library book. I drew on Ariel's sister, the mermaid.
Tiffany: Yeah, we're not supposed to draw on library books, huh?
Kenzie: I'm sorry library. I didn't mean to. It was an accident.
