Now, opposite of my last post. There are just some things I cannot buy new at retail price. It's just outrageous! Once again, some may disagree with my list.

  1. Children's books. Do you know that 15 pages of pictures, few words, and colors can cost $8-$17 per book brand new? I do value books and that is one thing I will always purchase for my kids. But I have broaden Kenzie's bookshelf collection with hardly used books from the thrift stores for $0.33 to $1 each. And often times, they are the same children's books parents buy from the retail stores.
  2. Bicycles. Since none of us are striving to be the next Lance Armstrong, we found second hand bikes much more reasonable. Nate and I both got our bikes from the Salvation Army for $8 each, and I got Kenzie's bike with training wheels for $4 at the thrift store.
  3. Cars. Mostly because we can't afford brand new cars. But also because Nate is the logical financial guru in the household who comes from a family that doesn't purchase new cars. It's the same argument you have heard in years, it loses it value once you drive if off the lot.
  4. Baby clothes. This one, I do purchase new sometimes. But when I can, I take hand-me-downs from friends or from thrift stores/yard sales. I find new or hardly worn baby clothes for much cheaper than retail. It is great because kids end up staining and growing out of their clothes so fast anyway.
  5. Halloween costumes. I have yet to purchase a costume for Kenzie. The store bought ones are nice, but I like the homemade ones because they are so creative! Kenzie's first Halloween she wore a onesie that said, "My first Halloween," that she got from a friend. Last year, she was a bumble bee - a costume that Grandma sent her. This year, I got fairy wings at the Dollar Store and made her a little pink and white tutu - she's a fairy.
  6. DVDs. Very few times we bought brand new DVDs. If we purchased it new, it was on sale for less than $10. Majority of our DVD collection came from Blockbuster's previously viewed section. We get movies 4 for $20. And since Blockbuster guarantee their DVDs, if we ever have problems with it being scratched or unplayable, we just exchange it. Our last trip to Blockbuster, we bought 6 movies for $21.
