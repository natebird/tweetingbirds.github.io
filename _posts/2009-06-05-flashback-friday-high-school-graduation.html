Attending Ashley's high school graduation reminded me of my own graduation 8 years ago (wow, has it really been 8 years already?!). I graduated #9 of my class with a 4.0 (it was actually higher than a 4.0 because I had IB courses, but colleges only accepted it as 4.0). Darn, AP calculus for lowering my cumulative average. I would have been #6 if it wasn't for AP calc. Enough with the brainy bragging.

I was excited to graduate from school because that summer I spent in NYC being a nanny followed by BYU-Provo in the fall. I was more than ready to move on with life.

Going through my graduation pictures, I realized that I didn't take any pictures with my parents at graduation. Huh, that's odd. I'll have to check with my mom on that one.

Mr. Gainey, principal, handing me my diploma. I have no idea where my diploma is today. As a class, we decided to all hand Mr. Gainey a lottery ticket as we shook his hand on stage. He had a little basket behind him to drop it in. He said he ended up winning over $500 from all the lottery tickets.
