I love having Easter with my family. Every year all of my family here meet up at my grandparents' house for food and an egg hunt after church. This year, Nate's mom had bought Kenzie an Easter dress a month ago. She looked so cute in yellow.

The night before Easter, I was at a Young Women activity. During that time, Nate did a trial run of the egg hunt with Kenzie in our living room. He said she did well finding the eggs.

Then after church on Easter Sunday we did a mini egg hunt with Kenzie in our backyard. This time she didn't do so well because after picking up the first egg and opening it, she realized this time there were candy in the eggs! She could care less about the other eggs in the yard. It took us 30 minutes to coax her to pick up four eggs. Nate and I picked up the rest. She was content with the candy she had in those four eggs. She ran around the yard holding her candy high in the air and yelling, "Candy! Candy!" Silly girl!

The egg hunt with Kenzie went much better at my grandparents' house. I guess seeing her cousins finding the eggs and putting it in their baskets helped her to grasp the concept of egg hunting.

Being the evil parent, I contemplated putting Cheerio in the Easter eggs. The thing is Nate and I are not big candy peopleâ€¦ I know, we are weird. So we don't have candy in our house. But luckily, I had bought some M&Ms; for visiting teaching and had some leftover. Even then I am extremely cautious of Kenzie's sugar intake, as my parents were with me as a child. So at the egg hunt at my grandparents' house, we gave away all of her candy but kept the stickers and quarters from the eggs. And no, I didn't pocket her quarters. I'm not that evil. I put it in her piggy bank.
