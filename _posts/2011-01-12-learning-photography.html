I have always loved photography. When I was in high school, I was in yearbook and newspaper class (I know. Shocking, right? Who knew I was going to study journalism in college?) and I got to work with film SLR cameras there. And then my high school teacher put me in a black and white film and black room photography class at Kennesaw over the summer. Loved it.

I also worked with a professional photographer for a few months one summer helping with weddings. I was his assistant that did all the running around for him. But I also learned from him. My parents bought me my first film SLR camera as my high school graduation gift. I still have it. It's a Canon Rebel. Been a Canon girl from the beginning of time.

In college, I took a photojournalism class. I thought about doing a minor in photography at BYU, but they didn't offer that. Only majors in photography and I couldn't fit it into my schedule.

Even then I still didn't learn the functionality of a camera and the concepts of photography. It was mostly just composition and stuff like that. I didn't really start learning the art of photography until I bought my first DSLR camera in July 2009. But when I got the camera, I wanted to learn to use it to it's capacity. Now a days, a DSLR camera is pretty affordable and just about anyone can have one. But people tend to think that if they have a nice fancy DSLR they will start taking better pictures. Totally not true. Sure, the picture quality is better than a point-and-shoot. But that doesn't guarantee that you start taking photos like a professional photographer.

I decided that if I was going to spend the money for a DSLR, I was going to learn how to use it properly. So last year, I learned how to shoot completely in manual. My camera stays on the "M" the entire time. Thanks to one of my favorite photographers, Nicole Gerulat. She has a blog that I follow. Her photography is totally my style. And I bought her Photo 101 textbook that got me shooting in manual. And at the end of 2010, she had a sale on her online photography classes. So I signed up for one. I started the class this week and it's awesome.

I also saved up enough money from my blogging to purchase a flash for my camera. I've got to figure out flash photography. I'm not a huge flash fan. I started a new Project 52 goal this year on Simply Modern Mom where I post a Glimpse into Motherhood picture each week completely unedited (the photo) to see how I am progressing in my photography. It should be fun!
