Thursday of last week, before Nate and his parents arrived in Provo, my best friend since fourth grade, Julia, and I took our kids to the North American Museum of Ancient Life at Thanksgiving Point. Unfortunately, the indecisive weather in Utah chose to snow that day in late April. The snow drifted down onto life on earth heavily. Unprepared with snow gear or even a warm jacket, we could only visit the museum and not the petting zoo or the tulip festival happening that week at the Thanksgiving Point Gardens.

It actually worked out fine because after wandering through the museum with two toddlers and a couple hundred elementary field trip students, we got back just in time for Kenzie's nap and before Nate pulled into the driveway of the place we were staying. Sorry to those who cares about the names and info on each of the dinosaurs. We were unable to read every little tid-bit on the dinosaurs because we were trying to keep our kids from running off, climbing on displays, or breaking the toys.
