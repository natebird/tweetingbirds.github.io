Thanks to Nate's technology savviness that he has passed down to our child, Kenzie has become an iChild. iChild meaning she can do the following without any help from the adults:

  • Knows how to use the iMac better than her mom because she knows all sorts of shortcut keys using the mouse and keyboard.
  • Can use the iMac controller to play photo slideshows, watch movies, and play music.
  • Knows how to turn on and slide the unlock key on Nate's iPhone. No, we are not rich. It's a business iPhone.
  • Knows how to rearrange the widgets on the iPhone.
  • Knows how to get to the photo gallery and slide across the screen on the iPhone to look through the photos or zoom in on the photos.
  • Can get the iPhone to play music off of iTunes.
  • Likes to use the iPod as a cell phone.
  • Knows how to plug the iPod into the speakers and turn it on the play music.
  • Loves use mom and dad's cell phone to call her grandparents or someone on our contact list.
  • Can identify the power button on the TV controller and turn it on or off.
  • Occasionally, when we say, "Let's play a game," she goes to get the X-Box or the Nintendo 64 controllers.
  • Can turn on the camera to view the pictures taken.
  • Knows where to plug in the cord for the camera to upload pictures onto the computer. There are two USB drives on the keyboard of the iMac.
  • Knows that you put the DVD in the DVD player for the movie to play. And she holds DVDs by putting her pointer finger through the hole in the middle, not touching or scratching the DVD.
  • Has a special attraction to anything electronic (very much like her father).
