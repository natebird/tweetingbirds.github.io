For some unknown reason, the bishopric had asked us to teach yesterday's combined Priesthood/Relief Society lesson at church. The lesson was on how to have successful family home evenings. So Nate and I came up with a list of 158+ ideas for FHE for everyone (singles, newly weds, young families, older families, and empty nesters). I'm posting the list we came up with for you to get some ideas for FHEs. They are not all original ideas from us, but this list should give you enough ideas to last you three years.

Family Home Evening Ideas:

  • Use church issued documents and manuals: Preach My Gospel, The Family: A Proclamation to the World, One for the Money, True to the Faith, Gospel Principles
  • Use church issued movies and videos (rent from the church library or order them online)
  • Make a meal together: fondue, BBQ in the backyard, foil dinners, progressive dinner
  • Bake: cookies, bread, cake, monkey bread
  • Go to the park: play flashlight tag, have an obstacle course, have a race, jump rope, have a picnic, play with sidewalk chalk, fly a kite, play frisbee, have a scavenger hunt
  • Learn a sport: soccer, basketball, baseball, whiffle ball, football, lacrosse, badminton, tennis, ping pong, rugby, skiing, snowboarding, swimming, golf
  • Paint, draw, or color pictures
  • Learn etiquette: answering the phone, setting up the dinner table, table manners, proper ways of sitting, how to treat boys and girls, how to respect adults, dating etiquette
  • Work out to a fitness video (rent one from local library or video store)
  • Learn about nutrition at the grocery store
  • Genealogy: make a family tree, fill out a pedigree chart
  • Have a talent show, learn a new talent, teach each other your talent
  • Share spiritual experiences and write them down in a family journal
  • Create a time capsule with newspaper clippings and something from each family member
  • Play get to know you games
  • Learn about the modern prophets and/or the apostles
  • Make up songs to help you memorize the articles of faith
  • Do a craft together: make quiet toys for young families in the ward, make a family quilt
  • Outdoor activities: biking, hiking, running, walking, exploring, bug collecting, bon fires
  • Make up a family skit or song and video record it
  • Design and make family shirts
  • Go through your things for a family yard sale, price tag everything, and make signs
  • Services: make cookies for your neighbors, pick up trash on the side of a road by your house, volunteer at the Boys and Girls Club, visit singles in the ward, plant trees
  • Go to your local library and research a topic or a country
  • Make up your own holidays: international day (each person present a country), purple day (dress, eat food, find things in magazines or books relating to the color purple), duct tape day (see what you can make using only duct tape), silent movie day (watch a movie on mute and the family members ad lib the movie)
  • Write letters to missionaries serving from your ward
  • Learn about numbers in the gospel: 10 lepers, 10 virgins, 10 commandments, 12 apostles, quorum of the 70s, the 3 Nephites, the 12 tribes of Israel, Noah’s 40 days and 40 nights, 4 corners of the earth
  • Go through family albums or watch old family movies
  • Have a service auction: each family member donate a service they are willing to do for another family member, write it on a card, and have an auction using Monopoly money
  • Cheapest buyer: Give each member in the family $3, they all go in the same store. See who can buy the best item with their $3.
  • Encyclopedia Smart: Each member pick a volume of the encyclopedia and skim through for 5 interesting facts to share with the family. Go to the local library if you don’t have a set a home.
  • Play board games (borrow some from friends or neighbors)
  • Learn a new skill: organization, filing, typing, cleaning, hair cutting, landscaping
  • Hometown tourist: visit local museums, zoos, tourist places, theaters, and so forth
  • Learn about different careers by visiting their work places, researching, and interviewing people in the field: local TV news station, local newspaper office, lawyer’s office, fire station, police station

Singles (of all ages):

  • Record your life history for your posterity
  • Write a letter to the future you in 5 or 10 years about where you hope to be and what you aspire to be
  • Learn a new talent or skill: woodworking, knitting, scrapbooking, gardening, piano playing, a new language, dancing
  • Start a blog and write posts each FHE night about spiritual experiences from that week
  • Find family genealogy names to do work for at the temple
  • Join or start a FHE group with other singles

Newly Weds:

  • Read your patriarchal blessings together and discuss
  • Design your dream house (draw or write about it)
  • Write predictions of where you will be in 5, 10, 20, or 30 years
  • Set financial goals: investments, savings, income, retirement
  • Discuss favorite memories from your childhood
  • Talk about family traditions you would like to start in your family
  • Collect marital advice from friends and family who are married

Young Families (toddlers through primary):

  • Learn Primary songs together
  • Look at the pictures and read from the illustrated version of the Book of Mormon
  • Play simple games: go fish, crazy 8s, tag, ring around the rosy
  • Learn about tithing using fake money or pennies
  • Role playing: cashier, ordering at a restaurant
  • Memorize your home address and phone number to a song or a rhyme
  • Hopscotch, freeze tag, Simon says, hide and seek

Older Families (teenagers):

  • Use the Strength of Youth Pamphlet
  • Learn car maintenance: change a tire, check the oil, changing the breaks, vacuuming and cleaning the car, rotating the tires
  • Learn how to fill out a check and balance a checkbook
  • Create and maintain a budget
  • Have a modesty fashion show
  • Learn about patriarchal blessings and the stake patriarch.

Empty Nesters:

  • Read a book together
  • Go watch a movie
  • Write a letter to each child on how much they have influenced your life and your favorite memories with them
  • Go to a concert, symphony, orchestra, play performance
  • Read the Sunday School lesson
  • Plant a garden
  • Have joint FHEs with other empty nesters or families
