The sewing machine, that is. I am so excited about my latest accomplishment! It's a nursing cover, aka "Hooter Hider."

I didn't really use a nursing cover with Kenzie. I had bought one from the store, but the material was too hot, I couldn't see Kenzie or anything under the cover, and no ventilation for her to breath while nursing. So I eventually just ditched the whole thing. Blankets didn't really work for me either because it kept falling off my shoulder or fall off anytime she moved.

With my second child, I'm attempting this Hooter Hider (yes, that's the brand name) that has received high reviews from many nursing mothers. I checked online for Hooter Hiders and the cheapest I found was $30. I knew I could do it for lessâ€¦ plus I had an itch from my latest obsession.

My nursing cover is a reversible one. And the cheapest one I saw for a reversible one was $45. I made mine for much less. Of course, it took a bit of time, but it was actually fun to make! I wanted a reversible one so that if one side gets dirty, just flip it over. So check it out! I'm so excited to use it!
