Looking at my second grade student ID, you may notice a big mistakeâ€¦ with my name. Second grade: my first year at Laie Elementary in Hawaii; met my first American friend, Natalie van Tassell (now Gardner); came to school not knowing a word of English; and began school winter semester, halfway through the school year.

Now back to the name part. My mom, recently remarried at the time, changed her last name to her newly married name, Boice. Well, I guess since in my school records my parents both had the last name Boice, my teachers assumed that it was also my last name. Sure, why not? Especially since I called my stepfather, dad.

Well, it turns out, my last name was Ping, not Boice. But since at school, my teachers wrote Tiffany Boice on everything, I wrote that too. It wasn't until THIRD GRADE when my dad (stepfather) finally noticed I was writing the wrong last name on everything. My parents had to rectify that mistake. It can be confusing when all of a sudden what you thought was your name was really not your name and you've been saying and writing the wrong name for a whole year!
