Starting this New Year, Kenzie decided she needs one of her stuffed animals to sleep with her at nap times and bed times. When it's time to sleep, I would ask Kenzie, "Which one of your animals or baby is tired?" Then she goes around picking the one animal who is tired. I only let her take one stuffed animal to bed because I don't want to have a whole farm in her crib. She has been rotating between her baby doll, her little Kitty, or her lamb.

But yesterday, when I asked her at nap time who was tired, she said, "Baby." I told her to go get the baby and the baby's night night (what she calls blankets). She got her night night then headed toward the stairs. I saw the baby still laying on the floor in the living room. So I said, "Kenzie, you didn't get the baby. Is the baby tired?" She responded, "Quack, quack." I was a little confused because she doesn't have a duck stuffed animal. I went back to the living room to get her baby. But when I looked at her, she showed me her duck who was tired. A while ago, my brother, Elder Jeffrey Ping, sent Kenzie one of those "Grow Your Own" ducks that you put in water and it grows up to 600% of its original size. That's who she wanted to sleep with!

For her nap time yesterday, I tucked that little miniature duck in bed next to her. She slept and when I got her after her nap, I found the duck on the floor next to her crib. She had thrown him out sometime during the nap. Here are some reenactment pictures of Kenzie and her duck.
